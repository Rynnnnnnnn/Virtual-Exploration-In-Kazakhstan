Welcome to Kazakhstan

Rich Culture & Heritage

Kazakhstan has a rich cultural heritage, with a bl Kazakhstan's rich cultural heritage is shaped by its nomadic traditions and diverse influences from Europe and Asia. The Kazakhs have a deep-rooted history of craftsmanship, hospitality, and preserved customs. The country’s cultural diversity is reflected in its various ethnic groups, each maintaining its traditions. Recognized by UNESCO, Kazakhstan’s intangible heritage includes yurt making, dombra music, aytys poetry, Nauryz celebrations, and falconry. Festivals and events, such as the Day of Culture and National Traditions, celebrate this heritage, while the country’s cities hold historical and linguistic significance.

Delicious Kazakh Cuisine

Kazakhstan’s cultural richness, vast deserts, geographical diversity, stunning attractions, and awe-inspiring attractions make it a popular tourist destination. Besides, there is one more thing that captivates visitors. And that is Kazakhstan food. The delicious delicacies of Kazakhstan drool tourists with their incredible taste. It is an exciting experience to dwell in the national cuisines of Kazakhstan. Most dishes in Kazakhstan include meat. Traditional Kazakh dishes are well-suited to a nomadic way of life.

Palau, or Kazakh Pilaf, is a beloved rice-based dish made with onions, carrots, spices, and meat, though it can also be enjoyed with vegetables. This flavorful Central Asian delicacy is widely available across Kazakhstan and is a must-try for food lovers. Its rich taste and aroma make it a popular choice on almost every menu.

Kuurdak is a traditional Central Asian dish, originally from Kyrgyzstan but widely popular in Kazakhstan. Made with roasted or deep-fried meat, garlic, onions, and potatoes, it is a favorite among both locals and tourists. As one of Kazakhstan’s oldest dishes, Kuurdak remains a staple on menus across the country.

Beshbarmak, the national dish of Kazakhstan, is a flavorful meal made with boiled meat, thin pasta sheets, and a savory broth-based sauce. Its name, meaning “five fingers,” reflects the traditional way nomadic people eat it by hand. A staple at celebrations and widely available in restaurants, Beshbarmak is a must-try for anyone visiting Kazakhstan.

Koktal is a flavorful Kazakh dish made from hot smoked freshwater fish, infused with sweet peppers, onions, and tomatoes. With its rich, smoky aroma and unique taste, it leaves a lasting impression on anyone who tries it. Though it takes time to prepare, Koktal is a must-try delicacy for visitors in Kazakhstan.

Manti is a popular Kazakh dish, especially among the youth, consisting of steamed dumplings filled with spiced meat, pumpkin, and black pepper. Served with sour cream and onion or garlic sauce, these dumplings come in various shapes and sizes depending on the region. A widely available and convenient snack, Manti is a must-try for both locals and tourists.

Shelpek is a traditional Kazakh flatbread made from dough with sour cream, flour, milk, and butter, then deep-fried until golden brown. Similar to Indian Puri, it is often prepared on Fridays to honor ancestors and served with cheese, jam, or sour cream alongside tea. A staple in Kazakh cuisine, Shelpek is widely available and a must-try for visitors.

Tandyr Nan is a beloved Kazakh bread baked in a traditional clay oven, giving it a smoky, crispy crust and rich aroma. Made with just water, flour, and salt, it pairs well with both savory and sweet dishes, especially meats like Plov, Beshbarmak, and horse meat stew. A cultural staple, this flavorful bread is a must-try in Kazakhstan.

Exciting Activities in Kazakhstan

From exploring the vast steppes to marveling at stunning mountain landscapes, Kazakhstan offers unforgettable adventures for every traveler. Discover some of the best activities to enjoy during your visit.

Hiking and Mountain Hiking:

Popular in Almaty Region, Eastern Kazakhstan, and Turkistan Province. You can explore the Ili Alatau Mountains, the mysterious Belukha Mountain, and the Sairam-Ugam National Park.

Horseback Riding

Enjoy horseback tours in the picturesque jailau of Jetisu in Almaty Province, or go further to Asy Plateau or Shalkodesu River valley.

Bicycle Travel and Mountain Biking

Join local communities or clubs for cycling adventures in any region of Kazakhstan. The Bicycle Adventure Club in Almaty offers guided tours.

Exploring the Steppe

Drive through the vast expanse of the Eurasian Steppe, a unique landscape with diverse wildlife, including grey wolves, saiga antelopes, and Mongolian gazelles.

Ice Skating

Enjoy skiing on the slopes of the Zailiyskiy Alatau Mountain Range at Chimbulak, the largest ski resort in Central Asia.

Skiing

Visit one of the most-visited attractions in Kazakhstan, featuring spectacular rock formations and hiking trails. You can also try white water rafting and canoeing on the Charyn River.

Top 5 Must-Visit Places in Kazakhstan

Charyn Canyon

Located in the Almaty Province, Charyn Often called the "Grand Canyon of Kazakhstan," Charyn Canyon stretches for 12 kilometers with towering rock formations reaching up to 370 meters. The canyon’s vibrant red and orange hues, sculpted by wind and water over millions of years, create a breathtaking landscape. The most famous section, the Valley of Castles, features naturally formed rock towers resembling medieval fortresses. Visitors can explore scenic hiking trails, enjoy panoramic viewpoints, and even camp under the stars for an unforgettable experience.

Kolsai Lakes

Nestled deep within the northern Tien Shan mountains, the Kolsai Lakes are a trio of stunning alpine lakes often called the "Pearls of the Northern Tien Shan." Surrounded by lush forests and snow-capped peaks, these pristine lakes offer a perfect escape for nature lovers. The area is a paradise for hikers, with trails leading from the first to the second and third lakes, offering breathtaking views along the way. The crystal-clear waters reflect the surrounding mountains, making it a popular spot for photography, horseback riding, and picnicking.

Turkistan

One of Kazakhstan’s most culturally significant cities, Turkistan is home to the Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Built in the 14th century under the orders of Tamerlane, this architectural masterpiece is adorned with intricate blue and turquoise tilework. The city serves as an important spiritual and historical hub, attracting pilgrims and tourists alike. Beyond the mausoleum, visitors can explore ancient ruins, bustling bazaars, and the newly developed Turkistan historical complex, which blends history with modern attractions.

Baikonur Cosmodrome

As the world’s first and largest operational space launch facility, Baikonur Cosmodrome holds an iconic place in space exploration history. It was from here that Yuri Gagarin, the first human in space, launched aboard Vostok 1 in 1961. Today, the site remains active, launching rockets and spacecraft for international missions. Mangystau Region

The Mangystau region is a hidden gem of Kazakhstan, boasting surreal desert landscapes, underground mosques, and striking rock formations. One of its most famous sights is the Valley of Balls (Torysh), where thousands of spherical rock formations create an otherworldly scene. The region is also home to underground mosques such as Beket-Ata, a sacred pilgrimage site. Stunning cliffs, salt flats, and remote canyons make Mangystau a must-visit for adventure seekers, photographers, and those looking for a mystical, off-the-beaten-path destination.
